# Code of Conduct

The Resonate community — across GitHub, Discord, and any official channel — is a place to learn, build, and ship durable systems together. This document sets the floor for how we treat each other.

## What we expect

- **Respect the people, not just the work.** Critique code, designs, and ideas — not the person who wrote them.
- **Assume good intent.** Most disagreements are misreadings. Ask before escalating.
- **Be patient with newcomers.** Everyone is new to durable execution at some point. Answer the question first; correct the framing second.
- **Keep it on-topic.** Resonate spaces exist for Resonate work. Off-topic threads belong elsewhere.
- **Use clear, professional language.** Strong opinions are fine. Personal attacks are not.

## What's not allowed

- Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination based on identity (race, gender, sexuality, disability, religion, nationality, or anything else that makes a person who they are).
- Sustained disruption of discussions, threads, or events.
- Sexualized language or imagery in any Resonate space.
- Doxxing or sharing others' private information without consent.
- Persistent unwanted contact after someone has asked you to stop.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all Resonate community spaces — GitHub repositories in the `resonatehq` and `resonatehq-examples` orgs, the Resonate Discord, conferences and meetups where Resonate is officially represented, and any other channel run by the Resonate team. It also applies when you're representing Resonate in public — for example, posting about Resonate on social media using a maintainer role.

## Reporting

If you see or experience behavior that violates this code:

- **GitHub:** report via the [contact path in SECURITY.md](./SECURITY.md) — the same private channels handle conduct reports.
- **Discord:** DM a Resonate team member directly. The team is small; a DM to any team member reaches the moderation thread within a day.

Reports are handled confidentially. We will never share your identity with the person reported without your consent.

## Enforcement

The Resonate team is responsible for enforcement. We follow the four-tier ladder from the [Contributor Covenant 2.1 enforcement guidelines](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/2/1/code_of_conduct/#enforcement-guidelines):

1. **Correction** — a private, written explanation of what behavior was inappropriate and why. May include a request for a public apology.
2. **Warning** — a warning with consequences for continued behavior. No interaction with the affected people for a specified period; violation may lead to temporary or permanent ban.
3. **Temporary ban** — temporary ban from any sort of public interaction in the community for a specified period. No interaction (public or private) with the affected people during the ban.
4. **Permanent ban** — permanent ban from any kind of public interaction in the community.

Decisions are made by the maintainer team. We aim to be fair, prompt, and clear about why we took the action we did.

## Appeals

If you believe an enforcement decision was wrong, you can appeal by responding to the original notification within 14 days. Appeals are reviewed by maintainers who weren't part of the original decision when possible. Appeal decisions are final.
